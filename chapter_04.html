Use <details> for sections that are initially hidden, such as required disclosures or complex technical explanations.

  • Credit risk is minimal (AAA rated portfolio).

4.2 Semantic Layout Elements (Report Structure)

Semantic layout tags help search engines and accessibility tools understand the purpose of different parts of the financial report interface.

**<header>:** Contains the main heading and bank logo.

**<nav>:** Contains primary links like Dashboard, Portfolio, and Settings.

**<main>:** Contains the unique content of this page (e.g., the full report content).

Market Liquidity Report

**<section>:** A standalone, grouped piece of content, such as a major sub-report. This section covers market liquidity risks.

Global Investment Outlook

**<article>:** A self-contained piece of content, like a published news article or blog post, that could be reused elsewhere. This article details the 2026 investment strategy.

**<aside>:** Content related to the main content but separate from it, like a **Compliance Sidebar** or related market data.

This is the main body text flow...

**<footer>:** Contains copyright information, regulatory links, and **<address>** data (see below).

4.3 Semantic Formatting Elements

4.3.1 Strong (`<strong>`) - Importance

Clients must acknowledge that capital is **strongly** at risk, and that the final decision rests with the account manager. The new wire transfer limit is **$50,000 USD** per day.

4.3.2 Emphasized (`<em>`) - Accentuation

The system should *never* allow a negative balance on a standard checking account unless explicitly authorized by the bank manager. This is a *critical* security protocol.
